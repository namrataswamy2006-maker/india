Maharashtra, in western India, is India's third-largest and second-most populous state, known for its diverse geography (Ghats, Deccan Plateau, Konkan Coast), rich culture, economic power (Mumbai), and historical significance, with Marathi as the official language, celebrating its formation on May 1st, 1960.
Geography & Landscape
Plateau & Mountains: A plateau with the Sahyadri (Western Ghats) running parallel to the coast, eastern slopes descending eastwards, with Satpura & Ajanta ranges in the north/center. Regions: Includes coastal Konkan, Western Maharashtra, Marathwada, and Vidarbha.
Culture & People
Language: Marathi is the state language; English and Hindi are also widely spoken. Diversity: Rich cultural tapestry with many sub-regional cultures. Festivals: Celebrates festivals like Diwali, Ganesh Chaturthi, and Gudi Padwa.
Economy & Cities
Economic Capital: Mumbai, the state capital, is India's financial hub and home to Bollywood. Major Cities: Pune, Nagpur, Nashik, Solapur are key urban centers.
History & Governance
Formation: Formed on May 1, 1960, from Bombay State, based on linguistic identity. History: Ruled by empires like the Mauryas and Satavahanas. Government: Bicameral legislature; has significant representation in India's Parliament.
Tourism & Attractions
Hill Stations: Lonavala, Panchgani, Mahabaleshwar. Pilgrimage: Shirdi, Nashik (Sula Vineyards). Historical/Natural: Ajanta & Ellora Caves, Konkan beaches (Tarkarli, Ganpatipule).
